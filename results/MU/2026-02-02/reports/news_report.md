This suggests a cooling but still resilient labor market, potentially easing inflationary pressures.\n*   **Economic Activity:** The Chicago Fed National Activity Index improved in November to -0.04 from -0.42 in October, still indicating below-trend growth but showing some upward momentum. Factory orders increased by 2.7% in November, and wholesale inventories rose by 0.2%. These figures point to some strength in the manufacturing sector.\n*   **Inflation:** Inflation data was a key focus. The US Producer Price Index (PPI) for December rose more than expected (0.5% month-over-month and 3.0% year-over-year). US December CPI came in as expected at 2.7%, with core inflation at 2.6%. The mixed inflation signals (PPI hotter, CPI as expected) suggest that while inflation may be moderating, price pressures remain a concern for central banks.\n\n**Europe:**\n*   **Eurozone CPI:** The Eurozone\'s flash CPI for January was 1.7%, its lowest since 2021, indicating a significant cooling of inflation in the region.\n*   **UK Inflation:** The UK saw its inflation fall to 3.0% in January, also pointing to easing price pressures.\n\n### Central Bank Decisions\n\nCentral banks largely adopted a cautious "wait and see" approach, emphasizing data dependency and a desire for clearer signs of consistently lower inflation before considering rate adjustments.\n*   **Federal Reserve (Fed):** At its first meeting of 2026 on January 28th, the Fed kept interest rates unchanged within the target range of 3.50%-3.75%. This suggests a reluctance to cut rates prematurely despite some calls for it.\n*   **European Central Bank (ECB):** The ECB also kept its interest rates unchanged at 2% in January.\n*   **Bank of England (BoE):** The BoE maintained its rates at 3.75% but hinted at potential cuts as early as March, indicating a more dovish stance than the Fed or ECB.\n*   **Bank of Japan (BoJ):** The BoJ raised its key policy rate in January, a move against the prevailing trend in other major economies, signaling a potential shift in its long-standing ultra-loose monetary policy.\n*   **South African Reserve Bank (SARB):** The SARB kept its repo rate steady at 6.75%.\n\n### Geopolitical Events\n\nGeopolitical tensions remained a significant theme, contributing to market uncertainty and influencing commodity prices.\n*   **Venezuela:** A raid on Caracas led to the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolas Maduro.\n*   **US-Greenland:** Renewed suggestions about US control over Greenland.\n*   **Iran Tensions:** Simmering tensions with Iran following protests escalated later in February with US-Israel strikes.
